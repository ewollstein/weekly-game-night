7 Wonders

Style: Competitive

Players: 3-7

This strategy game has the players act as architects of up to 7 classic historical civilizations, almost like a competitive Sim City or the Civilization video games. You decide whether to invest in infrastructure, military, or commerce. You can also build Wonders, as the name suggests, that carry over through the 3 Ages you play through, giving you unique bonuses. Gameplay may seem complex at first, and strategies certainly can be as complex as you dare, but turns being simultaneous means things go pretty fast so you’ll see things in action really quick to pick it up.

Players take actions by playing cards. Cards are drawn from a drafting mechanic where cards are drawn from the deck of the current age equal to the number of players. The first player gets to see all of the cards, keep one, then pass the rest to the next player until everyone has drawn a card. After everyone gets a card, you play them & all get to collect or spend resources depending on the card played. When everyone has had a chance to play 6 cards, the age ends. Once you reach the end of the age, everyone adds up points from the cards they played & compares military might to get their score. At the end of the third age whoever gained the most points has the greatest civilization!

Betrayal at House on the Hill

Style: Both

Players: 3-6

Betrayal at House on the Hill is a tile game that allows players to build their own haunted house room by room, tile by tile, creating a new house and game every time. The game consists of a deck of room tiles (upper floor, ground floor, and basement), three sets of cards for Items, Events, and Omens, six different characters, and a number of tokens to represent the players and various monsters or items.

During the course of the game, each player will use the stats from their characters to explore the house and uncover items, events, and omens (based on the text on each tile). After the haunt, the rules to the game and the previous strategy you had completely change based on how the traitor is revealed, and who becomes the traitor. Survive the night in the haunted house with the traitor to win. Or, on the flip side, if you become the traitor, defeat the rest of the party to win. Who will betray who? What evil spirit or creature is now after you? Will you survive or perish? Find out in the house on the hill.

The points you lose ramp up with each blame token, but if you're the one to defeat the Troll that round, you can give one of your blame tokens to another player.

Munchkin

Style: Competitive

Players: 3-6

Basically, this is Dungeons and Dragons “Lite”. Each player starts at level 1 and fight their way up to level 10. In doing so, they will have special armor, weapons, potions, and other things to help them along the way.

You start the game with both door cards and treasure cards; there are also two stacks of cards on the table, both doors and treasure. As the player, you put down any armor, weapons, or other items that can help you along the way - all using the same rules as most role playing games (1 armor, 2 hands, 1 headgear and so on). The main difference between most role playing games and this one is that this is a cooperative game, as well as a competitive game since you can attack other players to stop them from winning.

Your turn starts by you kicking down the door from the door stack that everyone can see. If it is a monster or a curse, that card takes effect immediately; otherwise, you keep it in your hand. You can then “look for trouble” (fight a monster card you have in your hand) or “loot the room” (draw another door card only you can see). One this order is complete, you discard your hand down to 5 cards and it is the next player’s turn. You gain levels by defeating monsters or by “gain a level” cards. This continues around the table until one of the players reaches level 10.

Roll for the Galaxy

Style: Competitive

Players: 3-5

Roll for the Galaxy is a dice and tile strategy game of building space empires. You control a populace that help you explore new territories, develop new technologies, settle alien worlds, produce goods, and ship those goods.

All players start the game with a personal game board, five white dice, a blind to roll the dice behind, a cup to help roll the dice, and a player marker. You begin by choosing two face-down starting tiles from the game box, these tiles start your space empire. You also choose two tiles from the bag for your initial setup & choose which technology to develop and which world to settle. Settling worlds give you more dice with different colors, each with their own theme, and worlds can produce goods that can be consumed for victory points or sold for cash. Technologies give you extra powers or rewards when different phases or actions take place.

Players take actions simultaneously starting with rolling the dice they have available. The rolls are hidden while you plan what to do with your dice. Players select one phase they want to happen, then arrange their dice according to the rest of the phases they rolled. Once everyone has made their decisions, each player reveals their dice to determine which phases will take place that round. Then everyone goes through each phase one at a time, using the dice they assigned to that phase. Once all the phases are resolved, place any unused dice from that turn back in your cup, use your money to buy back as many used dice as you can, then start another round. Play continues until there are no more victory points left or someone places their twelfth tile, then count up victory points & see who wins!

Sentinels of the Multiverse

Style: Co-operative

Players: 3-5

Sentinels of the Multiverse is a comic book themed multi-player, co-operative, fixed deck card game. This means each player chooses one super hero with it's own card deck and they work together as a team to battle the villain, who also has a deck of cards. Each round begins with the villain turn, followed by the players, and finally the environment. Villains have start of turn effects, play the top card of their deck, then end of turn effects. Players can play a card from their hand, then use a power and finally draw a card. If they don't play a card and don't use a power they can draw an extra card. Some heroes will also have start/end of turn effects. Environments play like villain turns.

Suburbia

Style: Competitive
